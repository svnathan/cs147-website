About





Socializing over meals can be complicated for people with dietary restrictions - so complicated that people will do almost anything to avoid eating in social and uncontrolled settings. Whether you devise elaborate plans to make social eating work, have simply given up or isolate yourself from your social circles we are excited to have metchew. Your health and social life do not have to be mutually exclusive, so stop choosing just one and pick both with meetchewthere. meetchewthere redefines social eating for those affected by dietary restrictions by building trusted communities of geographically proximate people with common restrictions and provides a safe space to create, host, and join social events centered around food. Now the only choice to make is who to eat with!





Find Food

Sick of cooking? Looking to change things up? meetchewthere offers a restaurant search feature customized to fit your dietary restrictions. Simply enter your search, check out reviews from other users with your same restrictions, and get out there and eat!

Join Events

Looking to have a more social dining experience or just meet people with dietary restrictions like yourself?. meetchewthere provides an up-to-date searchable list of scheduled events in your area that fit your restrictions so that you can search for, find, and join social events centered around FOOD!

Make Friends

Ready to take the meetchewthere experience to the next level? Do you have a new restaurant you just found and are dying to tell your friends about? What about an idea for an awesome meetchewthere event? Well don't hesitate. Use the meetchewthere event management system to plan and create your own event. We can't wait to meetchewthere.

Low-Fidelity Prototype

Sketches, Paper, and Post-Its, Oh My!

We refocused after the short-lived exploration of our future careers as filmmakers and tried our hand at product design. Unsure of the best modality through which to implement the concept we had defined in our video, we brainstormed a variety of potential implementations ranging from jet packs and a magic bus to an old-fashioned newspaper advertisement. We ultimately settled somewhere in the middle of these extremes and created a paper prototype of the mobile application through which we would attempt to deliver our solution. 17 carefully drawn paper screens and too many post-it notes to count later, we took our prototype out for a test drive with three diet restricted potential users.
